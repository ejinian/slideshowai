## Problem (PAS)

- **Header:** You know you should be posting on TikTok. You're not.
- Agitate (3 short beats, bold leads):
  - **Filming is a second job.** Scripts, lighting, takes, edits — for a video
    that might get 200 views.
  - **You don't want to be on camera.** Most owners would rather close early
    than talk to a ring light.
  - **Meanwhile, your competitors post daily.** The algorithm doesn't wait for
    you to feel ready.
- Solve: SlideLabsAI removes the part you hate. Type one sentence about your
  business. Get a slideshow that sells it without feeling like an ad. Post it.
  That's the whole workflow.

## How it works

- **Header:** From one sentence to a posted slideshow in three steps
- Steps (visual, not icon-grid):
  1. **Describe the post** — one line about your business, or drop in your own
     photos. (visual: the self-typing composer)
  2. **AI builds slides around your product** — hook, captions, and photos laid
     out in TikTok's native caption style. (visual: real slide tiles)
  3. **Post while it's hot** — publish straight to TikTok, schedule it, or
     download the slides. (visual: action pills)

## Differentiation (strongest angle — give it room)

- **Header:** Why slideshows beat AI avatar ads
- Rows (bold lead + one supporting line):
  - **They don't look like ads.** Viewers clock an AI avatar in half a second —
    and scroll. Photo slideshows look like the organic posts people already
    swipe through, because that's what they are.
  - **Your product isn't plastered on every slide — on purpose.** That's what
    an ad looks like, and ads get skipped. Your slideshow leads with content
    worth swiping through, and your product enters like part of the story — so
    viewers reach your CTA without ever feeling sold to. (User direction
    2026-07-22: never claim "product in every slide" — it reads as ad logic.)
  - **Built for TikTok Photo Mode, posted through TikTok's official API.**
    Native 9:16 slides, TikTok Sans captions — indistinguishable from content
    made in the app.

## FAQ (objection-handling)

   captions. Photo posts don't have an uncanny valley the way avatar videos do.
2. Do I have to film anything or be on camera? → No. Photos only. Phone pics work.
3. What if I don't have good photos? → Upload what you have, or let it pull
   licensed stock matched to each caption by an AI vision check.
4. Do I need followers for this to work? → (keep existing answer — genuinely good)
5. Why are my first posts getting almost no views? → (keep existing + guide link)
6. Can it post to TikTok for me? → (keep existing)

## Design notes

TikTok-native look: near-black base, TikTok Sans display, white type ladder.
TikTok red (#fe2c55) for the ONE CTA style; cyan (#25f4ee) only for micro-details
(caret, tiny highlights). No purple/indigo on the landing page anymore. No
gradients except two faint static color washes (hero top, closer bottom).
