The most expensive mistake in TikTok marketing isn't posting something mediocre — it's posting almost nothing while you polish. Distribution on TikTok is a lottery where quality buys you better odds per ticket, but **cadence decides how many tickets you hold**. Even excellent accounts see most posts do modest numbers; the viral outliers only show up when there are enough posts for outliers to exist.

## The working cadence

- **Week 1 (new account):** one post a day at most, after a proper [warm-up](/guides/how-to-warm-up-a-new-tiktok-account). Young accounts that flood get throttled.
- **Steady state:** **3–5 slideshows per week.** This is the range where consistency compounds without quality collapsing. One post a month teaches the algorithm nothing about who your audience is.
- **Ceiling:** 1–2 per day. Beyond that you're cannibalizing your own test batches — TikTok distributes each post to a sample audience, and stacking posts hours apart splits the sample.

Consistency beats bursts. Fifteen posts spread over a month outperform fifteen posts in a weekend, both algorithmically (spam signals) and statistically (each post gets a clean test window).

## When to post

Time-of-day matters less than the advice industry pretends, but the sane defaults: post when your audience is idle-scrolling — early morning, lunch, and evenings in **your audience's** timezone, not yours. More important than the clock: be present for the first hour to reply to comments, since early engagement extends the test batch.

If you can't be online at those times, schedule ahead — batch-create on the weekend, queue the week, and let the posts go out on time regardless of your calendar. (This is precisely what SlideLabsAI's scheduler is for.)

## The quality bar per post

The right mental model: every post should clear a bar of "would a stranger swipe past slide one?" — but not the bar of "is this my best work ever." Formats are repeatable on purpose. Take the structures from the [viral anatomy guide](/guides/anatomy-of-a-viral-tiktok-slideshow) and rotate them: a gatekeep listicle Monday, a before/after Wednesday, a photo dump Friday. Same skeleton, new skin, every week.

## FAQ

### Is posting twice a day bad?

Not on an established account, if the posts are spaced 6+ hours apart. On an account under a month old, once a day is the safer ceiling.

### Should I delete underperforming posts before posting new ones?

No — old quiet posts don't drag new ones down, and slideshows have a long tail: posts in our library regularly spike weeks after publishing when search picks them up.

### What if I run out of ideas?

Ideas are the most automatable part. Steal structures from what's already working in your niche (that's what our Trends and library pages surface), and let the generator produce variations — your job is picking winners, not staring at a blank page.

### Does posting daily guarantee growth?

No. Cadence multiplies the chances your hooks get tested — it can't rescue hooks that fail the test. Fix format first, then scale frequency.
