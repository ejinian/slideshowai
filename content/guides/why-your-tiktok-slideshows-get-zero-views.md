Nothing kills motivation like posting five slideshows and watching every one stall at single digits. The good news: 0-view and near-0-view problems have a short list of causes, and they're diagnosable in order. Work down this list — don't skip to "my hooks must be bad," because hooks are almost never the reason a post gets *zero* distribution.

## 1. Your account is the problem (most likely)

If your posts consistently get under ~50 views regardless of content, the ceiling is on the **account**, not the posts. TikTok throttles accounts it doesn't trust yet:

- **Brand-new accounts that posted immediately.** The classic. Fix: stop posting and run the [7-day warm-up](/guides/how-to-warm-up-a-new-tiktok-account) as recovery.
- **Mass-posting.** Several posts within hours on a young account reads as spam automation. Keep it to 1–2 a day maximum until the account has history.
- **VPNs and device hopping.** TikTok geo-anchors accounts; hopping countries or logging in from many devices tanks trust.
- **Recycled media.** Posting the exact file that exists elsewhere on TikTok (or across your own accounts) gets fingerprinted. Fresh renders of your own content are fine; re-uploaded rips are not.
- **Private account?** Check the obvious: a private account's posts are only visible to followers. New business accounts sometimes toggle this without realizing.

> Rule of thumb: if three consecutive posts all landed under 50 views, fix the account before touching the content.

## 2. The post never had a chance to hook

Once the account is healthy, distribution starts small: TikTok shows your post to a test batch and watches what happens in the first seconds. Slideshows live or die on slide one:

- **Slide 1 must work with zero context.** A viewer sees it for well under a second while scrolling. Strong image, short bold claim, visible tension. Our [anatomy of a viral slideshow](/guides/anatomy-of-a-viral-tiktok-slideshow) breaks down the exact hook patterns from 2,000+ tracked viral posts.
- **Caption stacking kills.** Ten lines of text on the first slide reads as homework. One punchy line beats a paragraph.
- **No payoff structure.** If slide 1 promises "3 things," slides 2–4 must each deliver one. Viewers who feel baited swipe away, and early swipe-aways end distribution.

## 3. The boring-but-real technical causes

- **Wrong aspect ratio.** Slideshows should be 9:16 portrait (1080×1920). Letterboxed square images signal low effort to both viewers and the algorithm.
- **Muted or missing sound.** Slideshows with trending audio consistently outperform silent ones — sound is a ranking surface on TikTok.
- **Posting then vanishing.** Engagement in the first hour matters. Post when you can stick around, reply to early comments, and use the app afterward.
- **Banned-adjacent content.** Medical claims, before/after weight-loss framing, anything money-guarantee shaped — these get quietly suppressed rather than removed. Reword the caption if your niche brushes against these.

## 4. It's a numbers game you're not playing yet

Even healthy accounts with good content don't hit on every post. Across the viral library we track, top creators' feeds are full of posts that did nothing — the winners are outliers by definition. One slideshow a week tells you nothing; twenty posts a month gives the algorithm twenty chances to find your audience. Consistency compounds: see [how often to post](/guides/how-often-to-post-tiktok-slideshows).

## FAQ

### Is 0 views a shadowban?

"Shadowban" is the community word for what's usually new-account throttling or a content-policy suppression. Either way the fix is the same: pause, warm the account like a normal user for several days, remove anything policy-adjacent, then test with one strong post.

### How many views should a first post get?

On a properly warmed account, first posts commonly land anywhere from 100 to a few thousand test-batch views. Under 50 on multiple consecutive posts is the signature of an account-level ceiling.

### Do hashtags fix low views?

No. Hashtags are mild topical hints, not distribution levers. Three to five relevant ones are plenty; thirty spammy ones can actively hurt.

### Should I delete my 0-view posts?

There's no strong evidence deletion helps or hurts. If a post embarrasses the profile, archive it; otherwise leave it and focus forward.

### Does reposting the same slideshow work?

Reposting the identical file usually performs worse each time — TikTok recognizes duplicates. Rebuilding the same idea with a different hook and images is legitimate and often works.
