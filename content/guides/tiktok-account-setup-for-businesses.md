Your profile is the landing page every curious viewer hits after a slideshow stops them. Most business accounts lose the click in the three seconds between "that was interesting" and "who posted this" — because the profile answers none of the viewer's questions. Set these once, correctly, before the first post.

## Personal vs Business account

TikTok offers Personal and Business account types, and the honest trade-off:

- **Business accounts** get the website link field earlier, analytics, and look legitimate — but are locked out of some trending sounds (commercial music library only).
- **Personal accounts** get the full sound library — and trending sounds are a real ranking surface for slideshows.

The common operator play: start Personal while growing (full sounds), switch to Business once link-in-bio traffic matters more than sound selection. Switching takes a minute in settings and doesn't reset your account.

## The profile checklist

- **Username:** your business name, no underscores-and-numbers soup. It's also your search handle.
- **Photo:** logo or face, legible at thumbnail size. Dark-on-dark dies at 40 pixels.
- **Bio:** one line of who-it's-for plus one concrete promise — "Meal plans for busy lifters. New guide every Friday." Skip the emoji wall.
- **Link:** one destination that matches what your slideshows promise. If a post says "menu in bio," the bio link opens the menu — not a homepage with six clicks to the menu.
- **Pinned posts:** once you have three posts that perform, pin them. Pinned posts are your shop window; the algorithm sends viewers, pins convert them.

## One account per audience

A gym and a candle shop can't share an account — and neither can wildly different content styles. TikTok builds an audience profile per account; mixed signals mean weak distribution for everything. If you serve two genuinely different audiences, run two accounts, each warmed and grown separately (see the [warm-up playbook](/guides/how-to-warm-up-a-new-tiktok-account)).

## Keep the account human

Everything in [why slideshows get zero views](/guides/why-your-tiktok-slideshows-get-zero-views) applies from day zero: no VPNs, no bought followers, no device hopping, no reposting files that already live on TikTok. Trust is the invisible asset — every shortcut spends it, and it refills slowly.

## FAQ

### Do I need a Business account to post slideshows?

No — slideshows work identically on both account types. The difference is sound library access and the bio link field, not the format.

### Can I use my personal TikTok for my business?

If your existing account's audience matches your business niche, yes — an aged, trusted account is an asset. If the audience is unrelated, a fresh dedicated account will outperform it within weeks.

### How do I get the link in my bio?

Business accounts get the website field at low follower counts; personal accounts historically needed 1,000 followers. If the link matters to your funnel now, that alone can justify Business mode.

### Should my employees' accounts repost our slideshows?

Native reposts (the repost button) are fine. Re-uploading the same file across several accounts is duplicate-media territory and can throttle all of them. If the team wants to post, give each account its own variation.
