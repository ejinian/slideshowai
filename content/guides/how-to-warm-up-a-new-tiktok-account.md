Most new accounts don't fail because their content is bad. They fail because they posted before TikTok had any reason to believe a human was behind the account. Post ten slideshows on day one from a fresh account and you look exactly like the spam farms TikTok's automated systems are built to catch — and the punishment is the 0-view purgatory everyone complains about.

"Warming up" is the fix: a few days of using TikTok like a normal person before you publish anything. To be clear about what this is — it's **community practice, not an official TikTok feature**. TikTok has never published a warm-up spec. But the pattern is consistent enough across thousands of creators that every serious operator does it, and the cost of doing it is a few minutes of scrolling a day.

## Why cold accounts get throttled

TikTok assigns every account a trust level it never shows you. A brand-new account with no watch history, no likes, and no follows that immediately starts publishing is the exact behavioral signature of a bot network. The algorithm doesn't need to prove you're a bot — it just quietly gives your posts minimal distribution until your behavior looks human.

The three situations that need a warm-up:

- A **brand-new account**, registered this week.
- A **dormant account** that hasn't opened the app in months.
- An account that just **changed context hard** — new device, new country via VPN, or a wiped profile.

## The 7-day plan

You're building a believable behavioral profile. Every action should look like a real person with real interests in your niche.

1. **Days 1–2 — watch only.** Open the app twice a day for 10–15 minutes. Search your niche once, then scroll the For You feed. Watch videos to the end — completion rate is a signal on both sides of the algorithm. Don't post, don't like, don't follow.
2. **Days 3–4 — start reacting.** Keep the same watch routine, and add a handful of likes on posts you genuinely find good. Follow 3–5 accounts in your niche. One or two honest comments — a real sentence, not an emoji.
3. **Days 5–6 — behave like a member.** Save a couple of posts, share one to a friend or to notes, keep commenting. By now your For You feed should visibly reflect your niche — that's the algorithm telling you it has a profile of you.
4. **Day 7 — first post.** Post one slideshow, ideally in the niche you've been engaging with all week. Then keep using the app normally after posting — accounts that post and instantly close the app look automated.

After the first post, ramp up gradually: one post a day for the first week is plenty. Our [posting cadence guide](/guides/how-often-to-post-tiktok-slideshows) covers the schedule from there.

## What NOT to do during warm-up

- Don't follow hundreds of accounts in a day — mass-following is a classic bot tell.
- Don't like everything you scroll past. Bots like everything; humans are picky.
- Don't run the account through a VPN that hops countries.
- Don't buy followers to "kickstart" the account. Purchased followers are the fastest route to permanent low trust.
- Don't post the same file across five accounts. TikTok fingerprints media, and duplicate uploads across fresh accounts is the spam-farm signature.

## If your account is already throttled

You posted cold and now everything gets 17 views? Stop posting for 3–4 days and run the warm-up routine above as recovery. Then test with one strong post. If a week of genuine usage doesn't move the needle, most operators get better results starting a fresh account (on the same device, without a VPN) and warming it properly than trying to resurrect a flagged one — see [why your slideshows get zero views](/guides/why-your-tiktok-slideshows-get-zero-views) for the full diagnosis checklist.

## FAQ

### How long should I warm up a new TikTok account?

Five to seven days of normal usage is the community standard. Three days can work, but the two extra days cost you nothing compared to weeks of recovering a throttled account.

### Is warming up officially required by TikTok?

No — TikTok has never documented it. It's community practice based on consistent creator experience: accounts that build normal watch/engage history before posting reliably outperform accounts that post cold.

### Can I post on day one if my content is really good?

You can, and occasionally it works. But distribution on a cold account is throttled before quality is ever evaluated — great content can't save a post the algorithm barely shows to anyone.

### Do I need to warm up if I'm just reactivating an old account?

Yes — an account that's been dormant for months has a stale behavioral profile. Give it 3–4 days of normal usage before posting again.

### Does warming up guarantee views?

No. It removes the artificial ceiling that hits new accounts — after that, performance is about hooks, format, and consistency. That part is what the rest of our guides (and the product) are for.
